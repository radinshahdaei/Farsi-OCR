  ص ↔ ض   |   س ↔ ش   |   ع ↔ غ   |   د ↔ ذ   |   ر ↔ ز

Arabic forms that should be Persian:
  ي → ی   (Arabic yeh → Persian yeh)
  ك → ک   (Arabic kaf → Persian kaf)
  ى → ی   (Alef maksura → Persian yeh)
  ة → ه   (Teh marbuta → Heh — Persian text only, NOT Arabic quotations)

Zero-width non-joiner (نیم‌فاصله / ZWNJ):
  OCR frequently drops or inserts ZWNJ (‌) characters. This is one of the
  most important corrections — incorrect ZWNJ changes word meaning and
  readability. Examples of correct usage: می‌رود, می‌شود, خانه‌ام, کتاب‌ها

Ligatures and connected-character breaks:
  Common ligatures like لا (lam-alef) and connected forms like محمد may be
  split by OCR into disconnected characters. Re-join them.

Diacritic and combining characters:
  Fix OCR errors in tashdid (ّ), hamza (ء), tanwin, and harakat (َ ِ ُ).
  These are often misrecognized or dropped entirely.

Misrecognized numbers:
  Persian ۰۱۲۳۴۵۶۷۸۹ vs Arabic ٠١٢٣٤٥٦٧٨٩ vs Western 0123456789
  Correct to the form used by the surrounding text.

Spurious characters to remove:
  Tatweel/kashida (ـ) — stretching character, remove everywhere
  RTL/LTR marks (‏ ‎) — invisible directional controls, remove everywhere

Scanning artifacts / noise:
  Scanner streaks, watermarks rendered as garbled text, repeated nonsense
  characters, speckle patterns. Remove these — they are not content.

Incorrect word segmentation:
  Persian is cursive; OCR sometimes breaks words at wrong points or
  merges adjacent words. Restore correct boundaries.
